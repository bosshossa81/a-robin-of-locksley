Gotham Love

Inspired by Tom King’s “Grayson: Futures End #1”

Fortnights spent beneath ragged gutters spewing acid
dreams. Underneath scoliotic braces of midnight trams
and domineering shadows of neogothic towers. Cast into
infested spillways that you call home. Kennel of Gotham.

Yesterday’s Man fell from the heavens into your infernal
cauldron of derelict seduction. Offered him your savage
game of cat and bat. Untied his truths, rent his justice.
Ripped cape left shuttering over his bloody rubble guts.

Gratitude: the junkie’s plea to the tenth avenue dealer.
Order: the blind mistress’ toss of double-headed silver.
Trust: the sharpened knife given to the convicted cheater.
Honesty: the secret, sacred desire of the serene deceiver.
Asphyxiation: the red-light embrace beneath exotic fur.
Machine Gun: the fatal heartbeat of the cackling victor.

Leotard pixie boots of yours prance atop downtrodden
rooftops like wanton ropedancers, netless. Open calls,
invitations to reapply the big top powder. Varnish the
eyelids with scarlet amber, buckle up the freshly sliced
roman numerals on the skin, spritz the wrists with spider
crawling toxin‒all for her sweet poison lips. Eternal bliss.
