Holy Throne

Amongst the wreckage of lives it sits.
Reigning on high, over the lost memories,
broken dreams.
The clouds crying out
horrors and screams.

Up there, pristinely preserved
on the pedestal of shattered glass
broken wood.
Nothing left,
it alone withstood.

Drops glide down its buffed crown.
Endless swirls towards scuffed shingles.
Broken loop,
melting away forsaken shelter
to a droop.

Herald horns, crashing cymbals,
thunderous treble, bombastic bass,
broken crescendo.
The audience forced to applaud
the piercing piccolo.

Church choirs exclaim,
“Was it the wrath of God?”
Broken faith
laid to waste
by the Holy Wraith.

Why must it survive while others perish?
Were they too bold, too garish?
Broken, prone
they plead for the answers
only known by the Holy Throne.

The rain drains the spirit,
towards the porcelain rooftop.
Broken, unfit,
are any who prostrate themselves
before the King of Shit.

About "Holy Throne"

This poem is one of my favorite poems because it was just dumb fun for a writing exercise. The prompt was to write about an object in an unusual setting. So of course, I chose a very logical idea that anyone would arrive at: a toilet on a roof.
