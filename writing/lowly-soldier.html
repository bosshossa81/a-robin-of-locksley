The Journal of a Lowly Soldier

3rd of Allegenn, 1020 ADE

King Rothleth ordered the garrison to be reinforced after his failed coup at the Kymérian Gala of the Solstice. The fool, thinking he could just send a few errant troubadours to simply walk into Kymér and butter up a few drunken socialites to commit a little treason against the Golden Lily Queen. Hah. I would have loved to see the look on his face when he first received the message that five new flamboyant poppies were blooming in the Kymérian bailey—neatly plucked and preserved for everyone in the kingdom to enjoy, especially ole Rothleth, whose name was thanked on the little placards of each bouquet.

7th of Augustus, 1020 ADE

Man, is it hot out. We’ve been stuck up here in this damn waste of sand for months now. The only bit of excitement we had was when Norman drank a bit too much with the devil and danced across the rooftops, blabbering about what he would do alone with Queen Ambrose and all her precious poppies. We all gathered in the square to revel in the merriment of the drunken ballet: the stumbling pirouettes with his empty flask, the heaving pliés next to the chimney, the final saut de chat off the temple, through the stars, out toward the endless desert beyond the walls.

25th of Val, 1020 ADE

Word came in today that a meager pack of troops was seen moving across the dunes on the horizon, a couple hundred in number. They began setting up a makeshift encampment along the flowering banks of the Tempter, that miraculous river that somehow flows amongst the desolation. But from what I heard from the commander earlier, there may not be any fighting. Rothleth has been desperately trying to win over the queen with his brand of “diplomacy,” which basically means he is sending her all his foreign treasures to acquire his next bride. For once in my life, I am grateful for that lascivious pork pie.

24th of Ymolt, 1020 ADE

For weeks we were prepared for death. The oasis encampment’s numbers have risen into the hundreds of thousands compared to our meager two thousand. No grains of sand remain on the horizon—only the glint of golden helms. Buried within them are the foundations for siege dragons with their launched hellfire. Drinking from the Devil’s water were massive elephants equipped with war drums that shook the keep when they first arrived out across the desert. Luckily, Rothleth was finally able to plant his seeds. The crier came panting into the fort, exclaiming, “King Rothleth and Queen Ambrose to be wed mid-Noventus.” Just in time, too. There would be no victory in open war.

2nd of Noventus, 1020 ADE

That pompous bastard! Sleeping with the Queen’s sisters? He’s doomed us all.

5th of Noventus, 1020 ADE

The drums have begun striking, parting the clouds with each POOM. The fortress walls have betrayed us, repeating the POOM. The young boys soil the ground with each POOM. Green flashes crash into the barracks with a POOM. Screeches and prayers answered only with more POOM. We fall back into the keep, bolstering the door with each POOM. The Kymérians march through the fort with POOM. The king sleeps with another princess on his POOM. We await the final POOM. All I can think of is POOM.
