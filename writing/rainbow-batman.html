Rainbow Batman

Monday morning your Batman wakes you up in your dimly lit, musty apartment, damp from the seeping Gotham rain. He’s wearing green. You go to work at Wayne Enterprises to count your boss’s green money while he gallivants the aisles with his Patrick-Bateman-Bruce-Wayne. You watch them spill their chartreuse on the marbled floor, groping the interns they lure upstairs to their penthouse office. Your green Batman blocks your vision, “Don’t waste your time with those scum. You see, Greed will always—” You lean back, looking over his shoulder as he continues his lecture. You can’t help but imagine pummeling their million dollar faces in until your knuckles are covered in their courtly blood.

Tuesday you stare at the red gloves of your Batman as he gathers the breakfast plates at your father’s house. To break the tension, he chuckles, “Don’t tell Alfred, but this French toast might be better than his cucumber sandwiches.” Your father ignores the compliment. He’s been alone for three years now. It’s the anniversary. You talk about the Knights/Meteors game and the possibility of new hopes. You ask if he’s alright. He just gazes out the window at the eternal downpour of Gotham, “Your mother always loved the sunshine of Metropolis.” He turns back to you. “I’m fine, son.” You embrace each other, but in the corner a glare catches your eye as you see his Flashpoint Batman meticulously cleaning the barrel of his .45.

Wednesday the high noon golden rays glisten off overgrown statue tops onto the golden boots of your Batman as you walk across the gilded pathways of Robinson Park. From the ivy bushes you hear a rustle, then you feel the poke. “No heroes.” Quicker than a locomotive, a Bananarang leaves your Batman’s hand. The knife flies off as the mugger collapses. His coveted packet of Laughing Gas, betraying him, falls to your feet. You are entranced by the whispers of the cartoon Joker smiling at you on the wrapping. “What would drive this poor child to—” but before your Batman can finish, the whirring of chains and desperate cackles of the junkie’s Batman-Who-Laughs intensify from the brush. You turn to your golden idol for his guidance, “Quick, to the Bat-Mobile.” You leap into the passenger seat as the nuclear turbine engine ignites. Flames spew behind as you race down the streets of Gotham.

Thursday you burn the roof of your mouth as your Batman blends into the rusted-tangerine cast brick wall of the café caused by the blinding sunset over the Atlantic waves. Silhouetted in the chair before you sits your best friend, who recently returned from deployment in the tyrannical deserts of Khandaq. He’s speaking in a language of profoundly poetic curses and vaguely homophobic slurs, but you ignore the details. Instead, you are fixated on the squared jaw and refrigerator build of his Dark Knight Returns Batman who, by his brooding Clint Eastwood squint, seems just as intrigued by you. Your orange Batman leans over, “Is there something troubling you, old chum?” You could have sworn that your friend originally had a Caped Crusader when he left.

Friday evening you gnaw on breadsticks, crumbs speckling your navy Tom Fords, while your Batman swirls the glass of his earthy ginger ale that complements his classic blue suit. You await the arrival of your girlfriend at your reserved Maroni’s table. It’s your anniversary. His cape ruffles with the incoming gust of sweat-inducing heat as claws caress his shoulders. Her Catwoman tempts you with her purrfectly intoxicating lies and curve-accentuating purple spandex before informing you that her owner was done playing your “childish game of Cat and Bat.” She nips the unopened Tuscan wine before blowing you a kiss from the doorway. His blue gloves now caress your shoulders, “Fear not the night—it never lasts. Dawn always comes.” If that was true, why can’t you look away as the purple figure fades into the darkness?

Saturday night you force him to wear the purple suit. He stands idle and lets you plunge spoons into his back, break the nightstand over his head, and force him to eat your shoes. In the commotion you pant and glance at your feet to see the packet of Laughing Gas you never realized you brought back from the park. You read an address and number. You place a lampshade over his head before blowing him a kiss from the doorway. You find the alley in the storm. From the darkness, a dealer grinning with silver canines emerges. You hand him the money as the rain turns red. A strike of lightning reveals the hanging bat above with piercing red eyes. You instinctively turn to your side, but all you find is this descended Vampiric Batman in his place. Towering over you, you freeze as the back of his clawed hand slowly descends your cheek. You drop it all and flee back to the comforting ruins of your apartment. You take the lamp shade off your Batman’s head before falling asleep on the floor.

Sunday you wake up in a puddle, surprised by the renovation of your now pristine apartment. Heading into the bedroom you see clothes on the new nightstand with a note: Meet me on the roof once you change. You squeeze into the green leotard, button up the red tunic, tie together the collared yellow cape, wrap around the yellow utility belt, slide on the green gloves, lace up the green pixie boots, and apply the black domino mask. You look in the mirror and finally see. Climbing up to the roof, you are greeted by your multicolored Rainbow Batman. “Are you ready for today’s patrol Robin?” He hands you a Bat-Rope with a grin. You reply, “Holy Life Saver Batman. I thought you’d never ask.” You both throw your ropes before swinging off the roof for your next bat-adventure.

Inspiration

Detective Comics #241. DC Comics.
