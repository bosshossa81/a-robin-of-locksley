Tennessee Sunrise

Thanks to Matt Rasmussen

The steer that Eli wrenched the bullocks from
struts forth to tend his herd,

protecting the pasture. Amongst swaying switchgrass
I cut him over towards Bea’s Brook rambling clear

which reflects dawn-light upon
both banks within that creek.

A few mockingbirds serenade, tenderly
rippling through Appalachian air while I

make out katydid-bass droning on white.
My appaloosa snorts

as a swarm of dogwood snowflakes
brush suddenly through

the hair of her mane.
I rein her in atop Willie’s Crest

while gazing down the valley
until the cotton recedes

but mountains emerge, peak upon peak
of tulip poplar underneath virgin daylight,

calling out for me to return always
towards those Rocky Tops.
