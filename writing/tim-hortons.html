Everything Happens at Tim Hortons*

I'm tired of looking at all these blank-faced, spiritless schmucks, /
thinking that any of ‘em are cool enough. /
Is it possible for love to only cost ten bucks?

I remember that day at Timmy’s surrounded by caffeinated maple-pucks /
when you emerged through the doors, my scarlet quartz in the rough. /
I was tired of looking at all these blank-faced, spiritless schmucks.

Messily I ate my philly cheese steak bagel deluxe. /
Miraculously you approached me, promptly cutting out the awkward fluff, /
proposing the possibility for love to only cost ten bucks.

We talked about your love for ginger snaps, Zorro belts, and Peruvian ducks. /
The spontaneous freedom of your fiery hair singed my hardened scruff. /
I’m tired of looking like all these blank-faced, spiritless schmucks.

We laughed as the lumberjacks discussed the hard wood in the bed of their trucks. /
We were never too old for that school yard stuff. /
Is it possible for love to only cost ten bucks?

Stoically, I told you when it came to love I don’t give any %&*#$ /
but your red curls smile easily read my bluff. /
I’m tired of living like all these blank-faced, spiritless schmucks. /
Let's try this love that only costs ten bucks.
