What Lies Beyond

I’ve seen it.

I stood at the precipice of it all and reached forward—not for curiosity, but for desperation.

The silent reflection gave way upon contact, rippling infinitely outward toward the stars before reaching back. It slowly enveloped my fragile flesh. Before long, the calm of the reflection returned, with me now inside, sinking forward—down into the natal waters of the universe.

All lights faded, all sounds muffled; all that was left was my empty body, floating amongst the void in the vast bacta tank that is nothingness.

All that I once knew or felt melted through the pores of my skin until all that I became was an empty shell that once was—merely a useless collection of thoughts and consciousness on a thin string stretched out across the cosmos.

Yet, amongst that deep emptiness, there was a strange comfort that provided a frozen warmth within, easing all thoughts, lulling all worries, making it easier and easier to give in and let go.

Then I felt its presence in the darkness, circling through the depths, whispering to me in its secret tongue, enthralling me to accept the emptiness and the answers it denied me.

I woke once more upon the shores of existence, curled up and coughing out the fluid that had flooded my lungs.

I picked myself up and walked back toward land, to the world where people pass me as strangers, moving ever forward like strands of light around me—and I just sat down and stared at it through every reflection, what they were too oblivious to see.

My God, I’ve seen it.
