Who Said What?

He said, “The dreams are happening again. Worse than before.”
She said, “Your strength is brought to you by the company you keep.”
He said, “You can’t trust anything she has to say. Not after she left you when you needed her most.”
She said, “You need to go out again and rebuild those connections. You aren’t lost, you have to believe me.”
He said, “I can’t turn them off, I can’t make them silent. It’s just constant.”
He said, “They all betrayed you just like you did to yourself. If they cared, if she cared, she would have been there.”
She said, “You are the hope this world needs. You can reach out your hand and pull those who need you up.”
He said, “I say forget them, forget her. Get back to the mission. Take up the sword, and pierce the hearts of the wicked.”
He said, “When I’m in them, the metaphysical is reality. When I fight, it adapts. These invisible hands are constantly molding my perception around as I desperately try to shape it.”
She said, “You must help those that need it. You must make them laugh. You must comfort them when they cry.”
He said, “You must wage the war on those that need punishment. You must cut off the heads of the serpents. You must break their armies to rubble.”
He said, “All I want is for them to be over. I just want to be real. To live.”
She said, “Just let the water run over you. Let yourself become new. Let yourself become whole.”
He said, “You can’t just wash it all away. No matter how hard you scrub at it.”
He said, “But then it happens. It ends and as I lay in bed, I can’t help but pray for them to return.”
He said, “Are you even listening to me, or is that melodramatic amazon putting that holy art thou bullshit in your head again. She’s the reason were here in the first place.”
She said, “Are you talking to that wrathful demon again. I thought you left him to wither after he brought the horrid corruption.”
He said, “Days are nights. Nights are days. I guess what I’m saying is that I need your help to free me from this waking nightmare, but you have to make the choice.”
He said, “That’s it, we’ve got to do it again, but you have to make the choice.”
She said, “There still might be hope, though you will have to make the choice.”
He said, “Kill her.”
She said, “Save him.”
He said, “Wake up.”
They said, “Just do something!”
I did nothing.
