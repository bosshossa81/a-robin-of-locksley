Winter Breath

a golden shovel after a line from Patrick Phillips’ “Mercy”

The treacherous revealer: winter breath. The /
pulmonary smoke from an action so little. /
The frigid confession of every living ghost.

Ever encircling, that collective essence of I /
billows up towards a harsh heaven that was, /
before floating back to the brim of a seldom worn overcoat.

St. Joseph’s rings ten. /
Ice forms on their visage. Who /
could melt it? For Lazarus, even He cried.

These mourners gather, embrace, howl out. /
Tout ce qu’ils peuvent dire c’est merci. /
Self-comforting lies meant for who they think lies within.

If they only knew my blasphemy.

My judgment was gifted and my folium was read, was known. /
To avoid provoking their divine infidelity, I withhold my voice. /
Instead resigning to this hilltop vigil. A simple mercy.

I am sheltered beneath this manchineel as /
snow falls. Kneeling before the sudden white cliff I … /
I am blinded. Alone. Knowing I only watched.

I am a rock, departing the paradise island I was from, /
descending ever downward, into the dark, deep, /
inescapable reality within me, only found inside.

Marching alongside an unconquerable enemy. /
Deceiving my mothers and fathers. /
Embracing frozen skin.
