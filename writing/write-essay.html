How to Write an Essay

Ok. How do I start this? First, I need an idea. But what to do? What to do? Instinctually, I grab a foam ball I have sitting on the floor of my room. Naturally, I start throwing it against my wall, lost in thought. I mean, there is so much I could talk about, but then again there is so little. There was that time I ran into a pole—that was pretty funny. After all, it’s how I got my Harrison Ford chin scar. Or what about that time I taught myself how to throw a playing card across the school gym? Maybe it was a credit card, now that I think about it. I lean back onto my yoga ball that lives in the realm between my closet and the middle of my already tight room. Now I’m trying to get the ball as close as possible to the ceiling. How close can one get to the heavens without crossing that threshold into the great beyond? That’s when it hits me. The ball, that is. I sit up reluctantly in defeat. I need something to eat.

ONE SNACK BREAK LATER...

There is one major generality that can be made about me if one gets to know me long enough. If I’m deep in thought, I’ll most likely be pacing around. My father stands in the kitchen making dinner, glances at me, then goes right back to work. “Jane, he’s walking around the table again.” My mother walks past my father after a long day of work to put her stuff down on the table. “I can see that, Glen.” Reluctantly, she probes the question, knowing the answer. “Jacob, what’s wrong?” Oh, I’m glad you asked, Mother. I have to write this essay for a class of mine, and I was just out here thinking of an idea of what to write about. But then I got to thinking what it might be like to swing through the streets of New York like Spider-Man, and then I fell down even more rabbit holes. Now I’m just walking around. Oh, you wanted my actual answer. “Oh, nothing.”

ONE NICE FAMILY MEAL LATER...

I’m back in my room. I need inspiration. Something that can capture the hearts and souls of the American people. Something to make them laugh. Something to make them cry. I search my library of comics, my stack of video games, my shelf of novels, then it hits me. I dropped a book on my head. Not unlike a block of wood that once fell from my attic as I was putting something away. It was a real Home Alone situation. Holding my head, I reach down to pick up my copy of “The Divine Comedy.” That’s it. I’ll watch Jerry Maguire. My movies are underneath my desk on a little shelf.

ONE BEAUTIFUL LESSON OF LOVE IN FILM LATER...

Jerry, you’re a genius. I need to write my manifesto. All of my emotions, all of my experiences, all of the senseless suffering. This is my chance to finally let it all out. I proceed to mimic the title page of Jerry’s mission statement from the movie along with a parody of his opening. I now have the start to my essay along with an idea. But what now? I need to take a shower.

ONE MAINTENANCE OF PERSONAL HYGIENE LATER...

Showers have this magical quality to them. You just sit there as the water flows across your body, and thoughts naturally flow. A cleansing that brings about your pure thoughts. So there I sit in the mythical rain, grasping for inspiration. It can be like my entire high school experience wrapped up in a day of my present in college. Like if Catcher in the Rye and A Farewell to Arms had a baby. Only this baby doesn’t die at the end of the book. I can include all the hardships of my depression, the rise I took out of it through volleyball, the relationship I hoped to pursue through the girls’ team, the inevitable fall I had when COVID shot down all of my plans my senior year. Then it can all conclude in this ambiguous ending of me trying to start again as I am forced to wrestle my inner darkness as the night ends, and I stare into the sunrise pondering if I will finally decide to live my life that day. It will be my masterpiece. BANG! BANG! BANG! “You’ve been in there for forty minutes! Unless you plan on paying the water bill, I would suggest you get out. And be fast, that dinner’s not settling well in my stomach, and I don’t think you want to be in here when I need to go.” I heed my father’s ominous threat and get out.

ONE QUICK DRYING SESSION LATER...

I sit here staring blankly at my screen. Ok, time to write the best essay of my entire life.

ONE MOST EXCELLENT WRITING MONTAGE LATER...

“I might not have been drowning my sorrows with a bottle, but I found that with a nice pair of headphones, I could drown out my sorrows for a while with music.” Now that’s a sentence. Too bad it’s all I have in this section. Plus, the rest of the eight sections are completely empty. Reading over the idea section, I take my own advice. I pull up my Spotify and scroll down to my rock playlist: a collection of rock’s greatest hits over its entire existence. If you have fifty-one hours to spend, knock yourself out. I’ll just put it on in the background, nice and quiet, to inspire my creative process and motivate me to get this done. I mean, it’s all here in my head. Now to put it to the page.

ONE INSPIRING CREATIVE PROCESS LATER...

“Mamaaaaaaaaaa. OOOooooooh.” Punch the air a bit. “I don’t want to diieee.” Turn back to the audience. “I sometimes wish I’d never been born at alllll.” Drop to the ground, air guitar in hand, to shred a solo. Feel the rise and fall of the guitar. Each note gracefully bending in a melodical masterpiece.

ONE SET OF A LIFETIME LATER...

It’s midnight. I’ve wasted the day away and have nothing really written. Time to face the facts. I’m never going to finish this. I then proceed to get into bed and fall away into the dream world as Jeff Buckley serenades me to sleep.

ONE CRY AND NIGHT OF SLEEP LATER...

The day starts like any other. Upon realizing that I have an extra thirty minutes I can squeeze out before getting up, I practice my unpatented technique for pseudo-sleep. You see, if I bend one of my legs with my foot square on the bed while my knee rests up on the wall, one minute miraculously feels like an hour of sleep. When the realization of my own laziness kicks in, I begrudgingly roll out of bed to put on my clothes and collect my belongings for the last day of classes before our little break.

ONE EXHAUSTING FIVE-MINUTE COMMUTE AND DAY OF CLASSES LATER...

I drive back home ready to rest, but all I can focus on is dreading the idea of writing this essay of mine. Well, I can focus on writing this essay tonight. I could write it now, but I’d rather go into the night with it and do my other homework now. Seems logical to me. What can go wrong?

ONE PROJECT AND ASSIGNMENT LATER...

That’s that. All my assignments are done. There’s nothing else to do except write this white whale of mine. No more distractions. I proceed to spin around in my chair aimlessly.

ONE PERSONAL CAROUSEL RIDE LATER...

Ok, now I’m dizzy. I fall to the floor on all fours, trying to regain some semblance of understanding of the reality I exist in. My head raises and I pick it up. I mean, why not? I’ll play a few parts. Who knows, it could be just what I need to get me started. I put the strap around my head, get my books and notes out, grab a pick, and plug in.

ONE REPERTOIRE OF GUITAR PRACTICE LATER...

I mean, theoretically, if I just practice Eruption slowly, I could get to the lightning speeds of Eddie’s solo. Until then, I’ll just stick to the Beatles and Dylan. My phone begins to buzz. I unstrap myself from my magical mystery machine and check to see what’s going on in the world of my cellular device. It’s a Snapchat voice call from my three best friends: Tom, the marine welder, who is the kindest human being turned hardened badass; Cole, the former Marine now enrolled as an Iowa student, who thinks he’s a hardened badass but is actually a softy that is the most caring of friends; and Joe, the Amazon worker that also attends Lewis with me, who is just Joe, the most loyal friend you could wish for. We’ve been classmates since kindergarten, but it wasn’t until high school that the four of us really got together. Since then, we’ve been inseparable—besides the whole two-leaving-for-the-Marine-Corps element. Anyways, I join the call already prepared for what Tom has to say. “Company of Heroes. Now. Go.” The call ends as quickly as it began. We all hop on our computers to play Tom’s WWII strategy game that he loves. He’s been wanting me to get the game for years; reluctantly, I finally got it once Joe and Cole ended up getting it.

ONE NIGHT OF TRYING TO STRATEGICALLY OUTSMART AI NAZIS LATER...

Three. Two. One. “Like that, another victory for the good guys.” The game works by holding objectives to drain your opponent’s points. Once they hit zero, you win, but you can continue to play afterward. This specific map we were playing on was interesting, as it was split in two by a river with only one bridge across. The whole game, Joe and I were trying to cross this bridge and hold the other side while Tom dropped troops from a plane to help us win the initial game. However, it was midnight, which meant it was one in the morning for Tom in North Carolina. “Ok guys, it was fun, but I’ve got training tomorrow. Good night.” Cole, who was absent for most of this game, was done playing since Tom left and proceeded to build his city in Minecraft. So, it was just Joe and me left. I proposed a single question. “We could take this bridge, right?” Joe had a simple reply. “We can definitely take the bridge.”

ONE ETERNAL STALEMATE OVER INFRASTRUCTURE LATER...

“Joe, we lost the bridge.” After two hours of constant barrages and repairs, we made an effort to cross with all of our forces. We were systematically wiped out by German artillery and aircraft. With no more support from our forces, the Nazis casually strolled across the bridge. “I mean, we could have definitely taken that bridge if we had help. Right, Joe?” Joe responds, “I think we definitely could have taken the bridge with help. We had them at one point.” I realized that it was already too late to start another game, so I bid Joe adieu and turned the game off. Well, that was another night wasted. What a surprise. I get up and hobble over to my bed. There I proceed to fade away. I mean, we definitely could have taken the bridge.

ONE PTSD NIGHTMARE OF A BRIDGE LATER...

It’s ten o’clock, and I am still reluctant to get out of bed. Just another ten minutes and I’ll get up. I roll out of bed at noon and stare endlessly at the back of my refrigerator, hoping to find the meaning of the universe. Of course, there’s only the milk for my Frosted Flakes. I put the Beatles on the record player and sit at the table, lifeless, consuming my meal. I have to write this essay today, don’t I? Uuuuggghhh. That’s when I get a message that I have to give one of my fellow coaches on the school’s women’s volleyball team a dollar for “Dun Dun” or “Done Done”. I prefer the former as it sounds like a “Dum Dum”. Before most games, the girls play a game of three-on-three where, if they make a mistake, they’re out. The last person standing wins. The coaches and I each pick a girl beforehand. Lorelee, the head coach, yells, “Done Done,” and the game commences. If your selection wins, the rest of the coaches owe you a dollar. Rudi, the assistant coach, won our last round. However, I rarely have cash on me, let alone a single dollar. So, I decided to go fill up my car with gas and use a ten-dollar bill I received for my birthday to buy a Gatorade and collect some change. Afterwards, I’d go to campus to give her my dollar. While I’m there, I should probably find a place to sit down and write this essay. No distractions. Just me in a public area trying to get it done so I can leave. My plan was foolproof. It was time to get going. Wait. Octopus’ Garden just came on. I’ll leave when it’s over.

ONE DELIGHTFUL SONG ABOUT AN AQUATIC ADVENTURE LATER...

I made it to the station and bought my twistable-top Cool Blue Gatorade, the staple of my childhood gas station requests. It’s probably the best invention in the world. All you have to do is twist the cap with your mouth and you can drink. Close it the same way and it doesn’t spill. I mean, it’s quite genius, really. I get back in my car and arrive on campus. I walk into the athletic building to find Lorelee and Rudi working with a recruit in the main gym. Out of respect, I didn’t feel like interrupting them just to give Rudi a dollar, but it would have been kind of funny. Alas, I have not achieved that peak of comedic genius yet, so I head up to Rudi’s office and place ol’ George in plain sight for her. After leaving her a message about this mysterious dollar’s origin, I walk back outside. Time to write an essay. I start walking across campus to find a spot. I could—but I could also just go home and write it. I’m just going to go home. What? We said we were going to write this essay on campus. I know, but you’re just a voice in my head. Therefore, you are me. And if we are having this conversation, it means that I definitely need some sleep instead of writing an essay. But... But I’m right, aren’t I? I guess?

ONE AWKWARD CAR RIDE HOME AND NAP LATER...

I roll around in my bed only to see my laptop sitting open on my desk, this essay of mine left untouched. I guess the nap didn’t really help. I’m staring at the ceiling when my mother walks into my room. I guess it’s six o’clock already if my mom’s home. “How was your day? Did you get that essay done?” When did I tell her about this essay? I’m really out of sync, aren’t I? “No. I haven’t yet. I’ll get it done tonight. I just need to work on an idea I had.” I crawl out of bed, gather some clothes, and walk past her. “I’m going to take a shower first to wake myself up.” Before I can open the door, she stops me. “Ok, but make sure it’s a fast one. No more long showers.” Fine.

ONE MAINTENANCE OF PERSONAL HYGIENE LATER...

I stand in the shower directly underneath the showerhead. Nothing. I’ve got nothing.

ONE SLIGHTLY DEPRESSING DRYING SESSION LATER...

I grab my laptop, sit on my bed, and prepare myself for this. I shake it out. I slap myself in the face a few times—you know, all those stereotypical hyping methods. I proceed to rearrange a few sentences. After writing about a paragraph or two, I lose control of my focus and fall for the trap of my phone. I haven’t checked my social media in a while, have I? I check the basic feeds before scrolling through the endless void of Instagram memes.

ONE BRAINCELL LOST TO THE STUPIDITY OF THE INTERNET LATER...

Ha. That’s pretty good. I’ve got to send this to the guys. I screenshot a meme about Batman sprinting toward the screen screaming, “I am a model of mental health!” I scroll through my gallery to crop the image when something catches my eye. Wait a second. That’s it. The missing piece. Why it’s taken me so long to do what I want with this story. I stopped on an image of Keanu Reeves I had saved on my phone next to potential hairstyles. To draw Azraelith, I need to practice drawing people I want him to look like.

ONE NIGHT OF ENDLESS CORRECTIONS LATER...

That has to be it. It looks like him, right? Something’s off, though. I just can’t seem to figure it out. I turn off my light and put the drawing down. Maybe some sleep will help me figure out the problem.

ONE PEACEFUL NIGHT OF SLEEP LAT...

The nose. That’s what’s off. I had it misaligned. Now his mouth’s too low. Let’s bring that up a bit. Good. I go to clean my hands from the pencil shavings. I walk back to my bed. I make the mistake of looking at him. His eyes. How could I have been so blind not to see? They’re off. There’s no denying it. I proceed to erase his eyes again, and again, and again. I’m going crazy. You have to stop, Jacob. Go to bed.

ONE NIGHT OF SLEEP LAT...

THE NOSE! It’s still off. I made it too long. It was throwing off his proportions. How did I not realize that the hair needs to be darker? Also, his forehead is way too small. So are the eyes. I was totally right, though. Before I knew it, there he was—Mister John Wick himself, right in front of my eyes. My own creation. It was beautiful. Except his left eye is still off. I erase it to find him perfectly drawn as a pirate. I look at my clock. It’s already three in the morning. You fool. Go to bed. So I did. I left Keanu there, missing his left eye.

ONE NIGHT LATER...

I wake up at noon. Why must I exist? Why must I have to do things instead of just sleeping all of the time? Life would be so much simpler that way. I roll out of bed to see him in a new light. Huh. Let’s finish that eye of yours. After a few other minor changes, he was complete. Perhaps I can still create—I just have to get over the hump. I need something to push me to have this effort on this essay. No. I don’t need to find motivation; it’s already been inside me all along. I can achieve anything I dream of. It’s time. I grab my laptop and go to the living room. It’s time to write the best essay of my life.

ONE NAP LATER...

Crap. I was so close. I find my father sitting next to me on the couch watching an episode of Gold Rush he’s probably seen already. “Look who’s finally awake. How late did you stay up last night?” I give my normal reply, “Not that late.” I sit up, pondering what to do. I have to stay awake. That’s when I look down into our one-step basement and see the exercise equipment. Bing-pot! I roll out the yoga mat and crank out twenty pushups like nothing. This is going to be easy. I grab some dumbbells and work on my arms. Next, I work on my core with some V-ups. Why didn’t I think of this earlier?

ONE CIRCUIT LATER...

Zzzzzzzzzzzzzzz. “Jacob! Jacob? Did you fall asleep?” Surprised to hear my father’s voice, I roll around to see myself passed out next to the weights. Come on. Not again. I’ve got to find some way to finish this essay soon. If not, it’s most definitely going to be the death of me. I get up and bring my writing equipment back to my room. I lay down and start to write.

ONE ATTEMPT AT WRITING LATER...

I mean, these are technically sentences. At least I’m somewhat farther in the essay. I need to take a break; my brain is fried. So I pull out my phone and go onto YouTube. Ok, I have to be careful. I’ve been known to get lost in YouTube holes from time to time. Just a quick little video. Maybe it will give me some inspiration. Most importantly, though, it has to be for twenty minutes. Tops!

ONE DRUNKEN RECITATION OF REVENGE OF THE SITH BY MEMORY LATER...

“Du Dun Du dun Du dun. DUUUN DUN du du da Dun du—” That’s three hours of my life I’ll never get back. It was a funny video, though. I close the app and look up at the clock. You’ve got to be kidding me. It’s already two in the morning. I lay back on my bed and begin to realize how futile my efforts have been. Later today—I guess now—I have to finish this essay. What other distractions could I have? I’ve got to get rid of all of them now. I look down at my comics. There’s a new Hawkeye episode, isn’t there? I pull up my laptop to watch the next forty-minute episode.

ONE EPISODE OF A FAITHFUL ADAPTATION OF A BELOVED COMIC OF MINE LATER...

I thought it was pretty decent. A bit short, but as a whole, I still love the series. I close the laptop and lay back into bed. Okay, no more distractions. Time to sleep. Tomorrow I write. No ifs, ands, or buts. Time to be the hero of your story. Save the day, big guy.

ONE DAY WASTED BY SLEEP LATER...

I lay around in bed all day. I try moving to the couch for a bit. It’s no use. I’ve got no choice, do I? I’ve done all I can, haven’t I, little tiny conscience in my head? Fine. I’ll give in. I sit down and begin to write.

ONE NIGHT OF WRITING LATER...

Done. Now that wasn’t so bad, was it?
